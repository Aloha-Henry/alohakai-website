The Perfect Flow

Start: Morning Snorkel at Anini

Begin your day at Anini Beach, where the protective reef creates the perfect snorkeling conditions. The water is clearest in the morning before any wind picks up, and you'll have the reef mostly to yourself. Park near the boat ramp for easy beach access.

Why it works: Morning light illuminates the reef perfectly, fish are most active, and you'll build up a proper appetite for those legendary tacos.

Next: Fish Tacos at Kilauea

Once you've worked up an appetite, it's a quick 10-minute drive to Kilauea Fish Market. They open at 11am, so your timing will be perfect. The grilled mahi-mahi tacos here aren't just good - they're consistently rated as the island's best.

Local tip: Get two mahi tacos with extra lime. If there's a line, it moves fast and is absolutely worth the wait. Cash gets you in and out quicker.

Finish: Lighthouse Views

With a satisfied stomach, head to Kilauea Point Lighthouse for the perfect digestive stroll. The visitor center has fascinating exhibits about local wildlife, and the panoramic coastal views are unmatched. You might spot whales (in season), nesting seabirds, or monk seals.

Don't forget: Book your entry on recreation.gov in advance - they don't accept walk-ups. $10 per vehicle, and worth every penny for the views alone.
